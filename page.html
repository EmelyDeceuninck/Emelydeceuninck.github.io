I have always been interested in unique faces, faces that stand out in the crowd. I went out in the streets and asked interesting looking people to photograph them. I would paint from picture in my workspace. I soon found out that I missed something, a connection between me and my subject. I decided to paint people that stand close to me, my relatives, people that I love...

People in space

I find it fascinating to put a part of a person on a painting that shows the person how he really is. Time passes and it occured to me that I missed the personal experience of meeting the person and painting them whilst sitting in front of me. The meeting between the artist and the portrayed one became very important to me.

Nature morte

What is it about portraits that fascinate me so much? I realized that it's the material, the texture of the skin and flesh that makes me capable of playing with oil paint, and making thick substances. I wanted an object that I could approach the same way as in my portraits, an object that had the same fleshy material, something that I could bring with me every day without having to check dates to meet eachother. I started buying dead fish in supermarkets, I observed them dissolve and pour out blood.

The strange contradiction is that I love horror movies but that I shrink when I see blood in real life. When I was a child I did horrible things to animals because I was curious and still learning. I would tear off the wings of a fly, I would kill all the fish in my grandmother's pond and kill bees with a hammer. My mother did not approve these things and taught me that it was cruel and not acceptable. Am I wearing a mask made of the values of society? Am I actually enjoying torturing? Have my boundaries become thin as ice?

I do cruel things to dead fish, cutting them open and sewing them together to see how far I can go. I want to approach this in a childish exploring way, not with the common ways to approach 'darkness' but with bright toxic colors.

My paintings betray your eye the first time you see them but when you look closer, all you can see is blood and horror.
