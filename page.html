My process (2017-2018)

Portraits

I have always been interested in unique faces, faces that stand out in the crowd. I went out in the streets and asked people that interested me if I could take a picture of them. I would paint from picture in my atelier. I soon found out that I missed something, a connection between me and my subject. I decided to paint people that stand close to me, my relatives, people that I love,.

People in space

I always find it interesting to put a part of a person on a painting that shows the person how he is in his natural habitat. Time passes and I found out that I missed the personal experience of meeting the person en painting them while they sit in front of me. The meeting between the artist and the portrayed one became very important to me.

Nature morte

I asked myself the question why I find portraits so interesting. I found out that it's the material, the texture of the skin and flesh that makes me capable of playing with oil paint, and making thick substances. I wanted an object that I could approach the same way as my portraits, an object that had the same fleshy material, something that I could bring with me every day without checking dates to meet eachother. I started bying dead fish in supermarkets, I observed them dissolve and pour out blood.

The strange contradiction that I found in myself is that I love horror movies but that I shrink when I see blood in real life. I think about the past, when I was a child I did horrible things to animals because I was curious and learning. I would tear of the wings of a fly, I would kill all the fishes in my grandmothers pond and kill bees with a hammer. My mother did not approve these things and thaught me that it was cruel and not acceptable in the society we live in. I am investigating if I am wearing a mask made of the values of society of that I am actually enjoying torturing and if my boundries are thin as ice.

I am doing cruel things with dead fish, cutting them open and sewing them together to see how far I can go with this. I want to approach this in a childish way of exploring, not with the common ways to approach 'darkness' but with bright toxic colors.

My paintings betray your eye the first time you see it but when you look closer all you can see is blood and horror.
